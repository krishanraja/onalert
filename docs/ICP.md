# Ideal Customer Profile

## Primary Persona: The Frequent Traveler

### Demographics
- **Age**: 25-55
- **Income**: $75K+ household income
- **Location**: Major US metro areas (near high-demand enrollment centers)
- **Travel frequency**: 3+ international trips per year
- **Tech comfort**: Uses apps daily, comfortable with SaaS tools

### Psychographics
- Values time and convenience over cost
- Willing to pay for tools that automate tedious tasks
- Proactive -- already applied for Trusted Traveler Programs
- Researches solutions online before purchasing
- Active in travel communities (Reddit, points/miles blogs)

### Situation
- Conditionally approved for Global Entry, TSA PreCheck, NEXUS, or SENTRI
- Facing 3-12 month wait for enrollment interview at a preferred location
- Aware that cancellation slots exist but frustrated by manual checking
- Has an upcoming trip that would benefit from expedited processing
- May have already missed a slot due to poor timing

### Pain Points
1. **Time sink**: Constantly refreshing the CBP scheduler (multiple times daily)
2. **Missed slots**: Slots fill in 5-15 minutes -- manual checking is a losing strategy
3. **Trip anxiety**: Upcoming travel without Global Entry, knowing the wait is months
4. **No alternatives**: Existing tools are unreliable, abandoned, or overpriced
5. **Opportunity cost**: Hours spent refreshing could be spent on anything else

### Decision Triggers
- "My trip is in 6 weeks and the next appointment is in 9 months"
- "I keep missing slots by minutes -- there has to be a better way"
- "I found a Reddit post about a monitoring service"
- "A friend told me they got their appointment through a slot alert"
- "I just got conditionally approved and the wait times are insane"

### Willingness to Pay
- **High**: $19/month is negligible compared to the value of getting Global Entry weeks or months sooner
- **Clear ROI**: Even one caught appointment justifies the entire subscription cost
- **Low churn risk**: Users stay subscribed until they book their appointment, then cancel naturally

## Secondary Persona: The Family Coordinator

- Managing applications for 2-4 family members
- Needs to monitor multiple locations and programs simultaneously
- Premium plan is essential (unlimited monitors, faster checks)
- Higher willingness to pay -- the per-person value multiplies
- Often the most vocal advocate (word-of-mouth referrals)

## Tertiary Persona: The Business Traveler

- Employer may reimburse the subscription cost
- Needs Global Entry urgently for frequent international travel
- Values speed above all -- 10-minute checks are the key selling point
- Less price-sensitive, more feature-sensitive

## Acquisition Channels

| Channel | Strategy | Priority |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **Organic search** | SEO for "Global Entry appointment alert", "CBP slot monitor", "trusted traveler appointment notification" | High |
| **Reddit** | r/GlobalEntry, r/TravelHacking, r/awardtravel -- helpful answers with product mention | High |
| **Travel blogs** | Points & miles community (The Points Guy, One Mile at a Time, Doctor of Credit) | Medium |
| **Word of mouth** | Post-booking referrals -- users who successfully book share with friends | Medium |
| **Twitter/X** | Travel influencers, frequent flyer accounts, CBP-related threads | Medium |
| **Google Ads** | Target high-intent keywords: "global entry appointment checker" | Future |

## Anti-Personas (Not Our Customer)

- Users who haven't applied to any Trusted Traveler Program yet
- Users looking for free-only solutions with no willingness to upgrade
- Users outside the US who don't need CBP enrollment interviews
- Users who already have their enrollment interview scheduled
