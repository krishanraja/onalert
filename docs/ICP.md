# Ideal Customer Profile

## Primary Persona: The Frequent Traveler

### Demographics
- **Age**: 25–55
- **Income**: $75K+ household
- **Location**: Major US metro areas (near top enrollment centers)
- **Travel frequency**: 3+ international trips per year

### Psychographics
- Values time and convenience
- Willing to pay for tools that save effort
- Tech-comfortable (uses apps daily)
- Proactive — already applied for Trusted Traveler Programs

### Situation
- Conditionally approved for Global Entry, TSA PreCheck, NEXUS, or SENTRI
- Facing 3–12 month wait for enrollment interview
- Aware that cancellation slots exist but frustrated by manual checking
- Has an upcoming trip that would benefit from expedited processing

### Pain Points
1. Constantly refreshing the CBP scheduler (multiple times daily)
2. Missed slots because they were checking at the wrong time
3. Anxiety about missing a cancellation before a planned trip
4. No good alternative — existing tools are unreliable or defunct

### Decision Triggers
- "My trip is in 6 weeks and the next appointment is in 9 months"
- "I keep missing slots by minutes"
- "I found a Reddit post about a monitoring service"
- "A friend told me they got their appointment through a slot alert"

## Secondary Persona: The Family Coordinator

- Managing applications for 2–4 family members
- Needs to monitor multiple locations simultaneously
- Premium plan is essential (unlimited monitors)
- Higher willingness to pay for the family benefit

## Acquisition Channels

1. **Organic search**: "Global Entry appointment alert", "CBP slot monitor"
2. **Reddit**: r/GlobalEntry, r/TravelHacking, r/awardtravel
3. **Travel blogs**: Points & miles community
4. **Word of mouth**: Post-booking referrals
5. **Twitter/X**: Travel influencers, frequent flyer accounts
